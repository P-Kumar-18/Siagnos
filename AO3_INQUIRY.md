**Inquiry Regarding Siagnos and AO3 Metadata Collection**

---

Dear OTW Research Committee,

My name is Priyansh Kumar, and I am a student from India. I'm currently working on a personal project called Siagnos, and I wanted to reach out to ask for some guidance before I take it any further.

The idea behind Siagnos is fairly simple. As someone who reads fanfiction, I've often found it difficult to discover works that match very specific tastes and preferences. I started wondering whether metadata alone could be used to build better recommendations without needing to access or analyze the stories themselves.

Because of that, the project only collects publicly available metadata such as work IDs, titles, fandoms, tags, word counts, kudos, and similar information. I am not downloading, storing, or processing the text of fanfiction works.

I've also tried to be careful about how the project interacts with AO3. The script uses a single session and waits eight seconds between requests to avoid putting unnecessary load on the Archive. I also intend to respect any access restrictions, including user-locked content.

I know there have been understandable concerns within fandom spaces about large-scale scraping and AI-related projects, so I wanted to be upfront about what I'm doing. Siagnos is not a generative AI system, it does not create content, and it is not intended for training language models. The goal is simply to help readers discover fan-created works they are more likely to enjoy.

At the moment, this is just a private learning project. If I ever decide to release it publicly, I plan for it to remain free, non-commercial, and open source.

I would appreciate any guidance on whether this approach raises concerns or conflicts with AO3 policies, best practices, or community expectations. I'd much rather understand those considerations now than make incorrect assumptions later.

Thank you for your time, and thank you for all the work that goes into maintaining the Archive.

Best regards,

Priyansh Kumar
